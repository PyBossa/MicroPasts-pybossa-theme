{{brand}}: Licenses

The {{brand}} crowd-sourcing platform uses the free and open source framework PyBossa which is released under the GNU Affero General Public License version 3.0 and all of the source code for the applications is hosted on GitHub.

For datasets, we make a distinction between source data and derived data. Our crowd-sourcing projects use digital source data (for example photographs, scanned documents) to allow contributors to complete research tasks such as the development of 3D models, photo-tagging and transcription. This source data will typically be available through a fully open CC0 license or a CC BY license, but in some cases might be placed under CC BY-NC or CC BY-ND licenses when the institutions owning the archaeological or archival material have asked that commercial use is prohibited without further consultation (we hope that this will be infrequent).

All the derived data, produced with the help of volunteers via crowd-sourcing, is licensed under an open CC0 or CC BY license, depending on case-by-case agreements with our partners.

Check the project's pages for further details about which specific licenses have been applied.
